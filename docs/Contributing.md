# 🤝 Contributing Guide  
## 🏛️ Federated Analytics & Insight Operating Model

---

## 🎯 1. Purpose

This repository simulates an internal Analytics Centre of Excellence (CoE) environment.

Contributions are structured to ensure:

- KPI definitions remain consistent  
- Governance discipline is preserved  
- Semantic integrity is protected  
- Certification standards are upheld  

This is not an open editing environment.  
All changes must follow governance review procedures.



---

## 📌 2. What Can Be Contributed

Contributions may include:

- New KPI proposals  
- Updates to existing KPI definitions  
- Enhancements to data quality controls  
- Refresh SLA adjustments  
- Stewardship assignment updates  
- Documentation improvements  

All metric-related contributions must align to the Governance Framework.



---

## 📝 3. KPI Proposal Process

When proposing a new KPI, the following must be documented:

- Business Definition  
- Intended Business Purpose  
- Declared Grain (Daily / Monthly / Composite)  
- Calculation Logic  
- Primary Source Table  
- Proposed KPI Owner  
- Proposed Data Steward  
- Refresh SLA  
- Quality Rule Indicator  

Incomplete proposals will not enter review.



---

## 🔄 4. Certification Workflow

All KPIs move through the defined lifecycle:

**Draft → Under Review → Certified → Deprecated**

### 📝 Draft  
Initial proposal. Not permitted in executive reporting.

### 🔍 Under Review  
KPI Owner and Data Steward validate definition and structure.

### ✅ Certified  
- Definition approved  
- Source and grain documented  
- SLA declared  
- Quality rule confirmed  

Eligible for executive reporting.

### 🗂️ Deprecated  
Replaced or retired.  
Maintained for historical traceability.

No KPI may be used in executive dashboards unless **Certified**.



---

## 👥 5. Role-Based Review Responsibilities

### 👤 KPI Owner

- Approves business definition  
- Confirms cross-domain alignment  
- Signs off on certification  

### 🧑‍💼 Data Steward

- Validates calculation logic  
- Confirms data availability  
- Ensures refresh alignment  
- Applies quality controls  

### 🏢 Governance Oversight (CoE)

- Reviews registry updates  
- Prevents duplicate metrics  
- Validates grain discipline  
- Confirms lifecycle integrity  



---

## ⚙️ 6. Change Management Standards

When modifying a **Certified KPI**:

- Version must be incremented  
- Change rationale must be documented  
- Impacted dashboards must be identified  
- Owner approval must be reconfirmed  

Silent definition changes are not permitted.



---

## 🧩 7. Grain & Semantic Controls

All contributed KPIs must declare:

- Calculation Layer (Fact / Derived / Composite)  
- Declared Grain  
- Aggregation behavior  

This prevents:

- Cross-grain aggregation errors  
- Composite misuse  
- Duplicate semantic representations  



---

## 📊 8. Data Quality & SLA Controls

Each KPI must declare:

- Quality Rule Status (Defined / Not Defined)  
- Expected Refresh Frequency  
- Last Refresh Date  
- Freshness Status  

KPIs breaching SLA must be flagged prior to executive reporting.

Transparency is required even when data is delayed.



---

## ⚖️ 9. Conflict Resolution

If cross-domain conflicts arise:

- Data Stewards review calculation logic  
- KPI Owners align on business interpretation  
- Governance Oversight updates registry and version history  

No domain may independently redefine a certified KPI.



---

## 📚 10. Documentation Standards

All changes must:

- Be clearly documented  
- Preserve consistent naming conventions  
- Align with the Governance Framework  
- Maintain registry clarity  

Documentation is part of governance — not an afterthought.



---

## 💡 Closing Note

This simulated CoE environment demonstrates how federated analytics can scale responsibly when governance is structural.

Contributions are welcome.  
Uncontrolled modifications are not.

Governance protects autonomy.  
Discipline protects trust.
